Payman is built for **two types of people**: those who use Payman wallets and policies to manage their own money, and those who build apps or agents that interact with them.

Users are individuals who:

-   Want to create and manage **USD or USDC wallets**
-   Set up **policies** to control how and when money moves
-   Add payees and **review or approve payment requests**
-   **Sign in to third-party apps using Payman OAuth**

If a developer has integrated Payman OAuth into their app, users can log in using their Payman account. During sign-in, users select:

-   Which **wallet** to link
-   Which **policy** should govern its use

This gives users full control and **guardrails** over how their wallet can be accessed or used by the third-party application â€” including spend limits, thresholds, and approval logic.
